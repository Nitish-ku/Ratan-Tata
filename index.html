Ratan Tata

1937-2024

Titan of Indian Industry

Ratan Naval Tata (28 December 1937 – Present) is an Indian industrialist and philanthropist, renowned for his visionary leadership in shaping the Tata Group, one of India’s largest and most respected conglomerates. He served as the chairman of Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group, from 1990 to 2012, and again as interim chairman from 2016 to 2017. Born in Mumbai, Maharashtra, Ratan Tata studied architecture at Cornell University and pursued an advanced management program at Harvard Business School.

Under his leadership, the Tata Group made significant strides in expanding its global footprint, acquiring iconic international brands such as Jaguar Land Rover, Tetley Tea, and Corus Steel, while also strengthening its presence in diverse sectors such as automobiles, technology, steel, and consumer goods. He was instrumental in launching the Tata Nano, the world’s most affordable car, and played a key role in transforming the group into a global powerhouse. Ratan Tata is widely regarded for his integrity, humility, and commitment to ethical business practices.

Ratan Tata's philanthropic contributions are equally commendable. Through the Tata Trusts, he has supported a wide array of social initiatives aimed at improving healthcare, education, and rural development across India. He remains deeply committed to social welfare, making a lasting impact on the lives of millions of people.
